Everest Camp Trek

Mount Everest is a peak in the Himalaya mountain range, located between Nepal and Tibet, an autonomous region of China. It is considered the tallest point on Earth, standing at 8,849 meters (29,032 feet). The mountain was named after George Everest, a former Surveyor General of India, in the 19th century. The Tibetan name is Chomolungma, which means "Mother Goddess of the World," while the Nepali name is Sagarmatha, which has various meanings.

K2 Trek

K2, at 8,611 metres (28,251 ft) above sea level, is the second-highest mountain on Earth, after Mount Everest at 8,849 metres (29,032 ft). It lies in the Karakoram range, partially in the Gilgit-Baltistan region of Pakistan-administered Kashmir and partially in the China-administered Trans-Karakoram Tract in the Taxkorgan Tajik Autonomous County of Xinjiang.
